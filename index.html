Invitation to Admire

E. V. Ramasamy (Periyar)

E. V. Ramasamy, affectionately known as Periyar, was an extraordinary social reformer and politician whose impact on Indian society reverberates to this day. Born on September 17, 1879, in Erode, Tamil Nadu, Periyar's lifelong dedication to social justice and equality was instrumental in reshaping the cultural and political landscape of South India. His pioneering efforts in dismantling the caste system, promoting rationalism, and advocating for gender equality have left an indelible mark on the nation's history.

One of Periyar's standout qualities was his fearlessness in challenging societal norms. He waged a relentless crusade against the caste system, organizing movements and agitations to eradicate untouchability and discrimination. Periyar's famous mantra, "Educate, Agitate, Organize," became the guiding principle of the Dravidian movement he spearheaded. His emphasis on education as a tool for social reform was groundbreaking and laid the foundation for empowering marginalized communities.

Periyar's commitment to gender equality was ahead of his time. He recognized the importance of women's rights and worked ardently to challenge the prevailing norms. His efforts led to the establishment of organizations like the Women's Conference, advocating for women's education, employment, and social and political rights. Periyar's influence extended to the realm of marriage reform, advocating for self-respect marriages that rejected dowry and embraced gender equality.

As the founder of the Self-Respect Movement and the Dravidar Kazhagam, Periyar played a pivotal role in galvanizing people against oppressive social structures. His speeches and writings, often sharp and incisive, inspired millions to question traditional beliefs and strive for a more egalitarian society. The impact of Periyar's ideology can be seen in the social policies and reforms that followed, shaping the political landscape of Tamil Nadu.

Even in his later years, Periyar continued to champion his ideals. His legacy is not just confined to statues and institutions but lives on in the hearts of those who continue to fight against discrimination and inequality. Periyar's life serves as a beacon of inspiration for all who strive for a just and inclusive society.
